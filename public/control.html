The five decks

Divide the standard tarot into five parts: (1) 22 Major Arcana, (2) 14 Chalices, (3) 14 Pentacles, (4) 14 Swords, (5) 14 Wands. Keep each deck separate and shuffle each separately.

Question

Formulate and visualize the question relating to the reading before drawing.

Draw and placement

From deck one (Majors): mix and extract five cards. Place as in the grid — Row 3: A (left), B (center), C (right); Row 1: D (left), E (right). From the four Minor decks: mix each separately, extract one from each. Row 2: F (above A), G and H side by side above B, I (above C).

Position meanings (A–I)

A — Past, beginning of the situation, or inertia: what is keeping the situation blocked and stagnant.

B — Present: development of conditions about which clarification is requested; how to observe the current movement.

C — Future: the conclusion of the entire situation or the balance that will be achieved.

D — Advantages: strong points to use and preserve, what comes to our aid, active influences.

E — Disadvantages: weak points to be careful of, what to remove ourselves from, risks, receptive influences for a new point of view.

F (Chalices) — Emotional center: feelings, influence of memories.

G (Pentacles) — Material center: economic situation, appearance, physical health.

H (Swords) — Mental center: thoughts, ideas, communication with the external world.

I (Wands) — Creative center: desires, sexual satisfaction, expression and creativity.

Closure

At the close of the reading, consult the advice of the dead relating to card B (present) for a suggestion on how to act and project toward a better future.
